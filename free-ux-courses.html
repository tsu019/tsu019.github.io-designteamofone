8 Free UX Design Courses

Whether you are looking for a career change or just to learn more about the field of UX design, these courses are a great starting point.

IBM: Design Thinking

Dive into the essential Enterprise Design Thinking skills: collaboration, synthesis, design research, prototyping, and storytelling.

2 hours

LinkedIn: Become a UX Designer

With over 12 hours of content, this LinkedIn course features courses from 13 different creators. Plus, you get a badge of completion for the ol’ resume.

12 hours

Accenture: Digital Skills – UX

Created by Accenture, this 3-week program covers the foundations of UX design, tools, and testing.

6 hours

Learnux.io: UX & UI Tools

Here’s a hidden gem that offers a plethora of free UX/UI content including courses covering the design workflow, prototyping tools, and even HTML & CSS.

20 hours

General Assembly: Design

Scroll down on the Free Learning Resources page to the Design section and you’ll find a list of free live-stream offered on a weekly basis.

Varies

MSU: UX Research and Design

Created by the University of Michigan, this program concludes with a capstone project, taking it from an initial concept to an interactive prototype.

72 hours

Georgia Tech: Introduction to UX

This course is for learners that have heard about “user experience” or “user interface” but don’t really know much about these disciplines.

6 hours

UC San Diego: Interaction Design

Learn how to design technologies that bring people joy, not frustration. Dive deep into how perception and cognition inform effective interaction designs.

120 hours
